Hello {{ user }}! {% endif %} Welcome to Systems Neuroscience of Auditory Perception Lab (SNAPlab)'s online portal! SNAPlab is part of the Department of Speech, Language & Hearing Sciences , and the Weldon School of Biomedical Engineering at Purdue University. At SNAPlab, we study the biological computations and neural circuits that underlie auditory perception. In particular, we are interested in how our ears and brains help us listen in complex everyday environments such as crowded restaurants and busy streets. This website is used by members of SNAPlab to host online listening experiments.

We are still in the development and testing phase of this website. Note that a login is required to be able to access most parts of this site, but you can easily register for an account in a few clicks by visiting the registration page.

Experimenter Area

SNAPlab members can visit the "my tasks" page to view a list of your behavioral tasks or create new ones. Note that "experimenter" credentials are required to be able to create tasks.
